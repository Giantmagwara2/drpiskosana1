FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services.

We recommend visiting the dentist for a check-up and cleaning every six months. However, some patients may need more frequent visits depending on their oral health needs. Dr. Skosana will recommend the appropriate schedule for you.

Restorative dentistry focuses on repairing damaged teeth and restoring their function, while cosmetic dentistry primarily aims to improve the appearance of your smile. Many procedures, such as dental crowns and implants, serve both purposes by restoring function and enhancing aesthetics.

With proper care and maintenance, dental implants can last a lifetime. The crown attached to the implant typically lasts 10-15 years before needing replacement due to normal wear and tear. Regular dental check-ups and good oral hygiene are essential for the longevity of your implants.

Professional teeth whitening performed by a dentist is safe for most patients. We use high-quality products and customize the treatment to your specific needs to minimize sensitivity and ensure optimal results. However, whitening may not be recommended for patients with certain dental conditions or extensive restorations.

Children should have their first dental visit by the age of one or within six months after their first tooth erupts. Early dental visits help establish good oral hygiene habits, monitor development, and prevent potential issues.
